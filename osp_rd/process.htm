Obtain Institutional Tracking Number

Obtain institutional proposal tracking number (PD, PT, or SC#, as applicable) by using the Advance PT/SC Request Form.

Comply with Deadlines

To comply with university policy, all proposal submissions shall be delivered to OSP for review and approval five (5) business days before the proposal is due to the Sponsor. OSP staff reviews proposals in the order in which they are received. Proposals not meeting these timelines will be reviewed after proposals meeting the timelines have been reviewed and approved.

Internal Approval Form (IAF)

The IAF identifies all relevant data about the project: who, what, where, when and how much. It documents the department and school’s review of science and the signature confirms commitment of human, lab space, or other resources needed for the project.

Financial Interest Report (FIR)

VCU’s Conflict of Interest policy applies to all proposals regardless of research funding or source. The policy requires the PI and all PI-designated "COI Investigators" listed on proposals (or protocols submitted to the IRB or IACUC) to complete financial interests reporting (FIR) and identify research relatedness via the VCU AIRS on an annual basis and within 30 days of discovering or acquiring an additional financial interest. An up-to-date FIR is required for all COI Investigators prior to proposal submission.

Routing

All proposals to external sponsors must be reviewed and approved by a PI's college/school dean and central OSP's authorized official. Every proposal is "routed" through each level and approval is captured through signature. College/school signature commits human and other resources to the project if funded. OSP authorized official signature conveys the university's support of the project to the sponsor.

All proposals to external sponsors must be reviewed by OSP prior to submission to the potential sponsor so that OSP can ensure that (1) the college/school has committed resources necessary for the project; (2) the proposal is compliant with University policies, sponsor guidelines, and federal and state regulations; and (3) the budget is accurate and uses the most current F&A costs and fringe benefit rates.

  • Route a paper proposal (hard copy)
    Utilize when your sponsor requires a hard copy, paper proposal application.
  • Route an electronic VCUeRA/InfoEd proposal
    Utilize when your sponsor is NIH, AHRQ, or FDA and is a single project.
  • Route a non-VCUeRA electronic proposal
    Utilize for all proposal applications that are submitted in whole or in part electronically (that are not submitted via VCUeRA/InfoEd)

What happens when OSP receives my proposal?

All incoming proposals are logged into the VCUeRA database by OSP's Intake & Records team and evaluated for OSP team assignment (Blue, Green, Gold or Red) based on (1) type of sponsor and (2) VCU school/college/center responsible for fiscal management.  A team reviewer evaluates all documentation for the funding application and requests any changes or updates based on (1) sponsor requirements; (2) compliance with university, state, or federal policies, procedures or guidelines relating to administrative, fiscal and scientific information in the application. Once all concerns are resolved, the reviewer obtains VCU Authorized Official signature.  Signed proposal documents are returned to the PI if the PI is responsible for submission. Proposals are submitted by the OSP reviewer if OSP is responsible for submission (most common with electronically-submitted proposals.)
