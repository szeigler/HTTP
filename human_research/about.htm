About the VCU IRB

VCU identifies on its Federalwide Assurance (FWA) with the Office for Human Research Protections (OHRP) 7 IRBs that routinely review research conducted under its institutional affiliation. Four of these IRBs are operated internally at VCU; 3 primarily review biomedical research and 1 focuses on social/behavioral/educational research. The remaining 3 IRBs are independent IRBs designated to review specific types of research.

Western IRB, a fully independent IRB, is designated to review industry sponsored research that will be conducted at VCU. The National Cancer Institute (NCI) Adult Central and Pediatric Central IRBs are designated to review applicable oncology related research.

The VCU Health System holds a separate FWA. IRB oversight for research conducted under the auspices of the VCUHS is provided by VCU.

The VCU Vice President for Research is the Institutional Official responsible for the IRBs. Administrative support to the IRB is provided by the Office of Research Subjects Protection (ORSP. The IRB Leadership and Enhancement Committee (ILEC) provides oversight to the IRB system. The membership of the ILEC is comprised of IRB Chairs, Vice Chairs, IRB administrative leadership, an unaffiliated IRB member, investigator representatives, and the research subject advocate.
