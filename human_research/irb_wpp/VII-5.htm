Section VII:

General Operations

WPP #: VII-5

Title 5:

Appeal of IRB Decisions

Effective Date:

09-1-09

Revision History:

06-20-00; 09-20-01; 06-07-04; 04-30-06; 06-21-06

Objective

To describe the policies and procedures of the VCU IRB pertaining to the VCU IRB appeal process.

Description

It is the IRB’s primary charge to protect human subjects while adhering to federal and state regulations and university policies and procedures. In so doing, an IRB panel may require changes necessary to secure approval which are not congruent with investigators’ planned procedures or documents. It is, therefore, critical that each step of the review process involve an open discussion and negotiation between the investigators and the IRB panel (informal discussions and consultations should occur as necessary). Directors of ORSP and ORCE, the Senior IRB Chair, the Panel Chair, and the IRB Leadership and Enhancement Committee serve as resources for investigators and reviewers throughout the review of a protocol.

  1. Appeal Process (also see flowchart at the end of this WPP):

    For exempt and expedited reviews: The reviewer will work with the investigator to resolve issues. If resolution is unsuccessful, the issue will be referred to the Full Board for formal discussion and deliberation. (For issues involving review by the Exempt Panel, the protocol will be scheduled for review by a fully-constituted IRB Panel). The investigator will receive a formal letter documenting the outcome of Panel deliberation and may also, as appropriate, be contacted by email or phone by the reviewer(s) or Chair/Vice Chair. The reviewers and Chair should work with the investigator to achieve a plan that meets both the study’s needs while adhering to the ethics of human subjects protection, federal regulations, and VCU policies and procedures. The panel or investigator may request an informal discussion of the issue with the Senior Chair, IRB Leadership and Enhancement Committee, or Directors of the ORCE or ORSP.

    For full board reviews: If a protocol is tabled or disapproved at the IRB Panel meeting, the investigator should work with the reviewers and, as needed, the Chair to resolve identified issues. The investigator may be invited to attend the next meeting after the protocol is resubmitted with changes. At that time the panel can discuss with the investigator any unresolved issues and obtain clarifications as needed. The investigator will be asked to step out of the meeting during the Panel discussion and voting process, but will be invited back into the room to answer additional questions and to receive the verbal report of the Panel’s determination. If the investigator and Panel cannot reach an agreement the investigator may contact the IRB Leadership and Enhancement Committee and request an appeal.

  2. Appeal Procedures:

    The investigator will complete a VCU IRB Appeal Form outlining the decision(s) being appealed and providing information supporting his or her position. The form will be reviewed by the Senior Chair and referred to the IRB Leadership and Enhancement Committee Appeal Process.

    The Director of the ORCE will serve as Chair for the Appeals Committee. The Appeals Committee will be comprised of the following voting members: Chairs/Vice Chairs from the uninvolved Panels, a non affiliate member from one of the IRB panels not involved with the review under appeal, the GCRC patient advocate, the Director of the ORSP, and a member to be selected by the investigator. This member, if possible, will be chosen from current or past IRB members, and, unless they are a non-affiliated member of an IRB panel, he/she must be a VCU employee. A minimum of 6 voting individuals must be present at the meeting. Unless waived by the investigator, the voting member selected by the investigator must be present (in person or via telephone) at the meeting. The IO may be present at the meeting as an observer and may provide information relevant to the appeal, but is not a voting member.

    At the meeting, the committee will focus on the unresolved issue(s), but will review the issue(s) in the context of the entire project. The investigator will present the protocol and issue(s) at hand. Consultants (as approved by the Chair of the Appeals Committee) may be invited to present relevant information, background, or precedent in regard to the issue(s). The Panel Chair/Vice Chair and/or Panel reviewers will present relevant information from the Panel’s prior discussions and decisions. After hearing the information and reviewing the documents, the investigator(s), consultants and those not a part of the Appeals Committee will be excused for the discussion and voting by the Committee.

    The Appeals Committee will reach a final decision by majority vote to either agree or disagree with the IRB panel decision regarding the procedure, wording, or plan as proposed by the investigator. The following decisions may be rendered by the Appeals Committee:

    • If the Appeals Committee disagrees with the decision of the IRB Panel, the protocol will be transferred to another IRB Panel for full review.
    • If the Appeals Committee is in agreement, the decision of the IRB Panel of record will stand. For example, if the original decision involved disapproval of the entire protocol, and the Appeals Committee is in agreement with the original decision, the protocol will continue to be disapproved by the IRB. The investigator may submit a new protocol with major revisions, aimed at resolving the human subjects’ issue(s), if disapproval occurred at initial review.
    • If the decision involves only a portion of the protocol (for example, recruitment), or a modification to a previously approved protocol, the Appeals Committee will refer the protocol back to the IRB Panel of record for review. The investigator or panel may request that the study be referred to another Panel. The Appeals Committee will have final decision regarding transfer of the protocol to a new Panel.

    In all cases, the findings of the Appeals Committee will be provided to the investigator and IRB Panel(s) in writing. The Institutional Official and investigator’s Chairperson will be copied on the written communication. The minutes and the letter will become part of the IRB file.

    All decisions by the Appeals Committee are final and cannot be appealed. The IO can not reverse a disapproval by an IRB Panel nor reverse the agreement with that disapproval by the Appeals Committee. However, if the Appeals Committee disagrees with the original IRB panel and the study is referred to another panel which approves of the study, the IO has final say and can disapprove the study.

  3. Evaluation of the Appeals Process

    After final disposition of a case prompting an Appeals Process, the IRB Leadership and Enhancement Committee will review the case and its findings in light of current policies and procedures to determine whether clarifications, changes in practice, new guidelines or WPPs are needed.

Responsibility

It is the responsibility of the ORSP to ensure that any requests for appeal are presented to the VCU IRB as part of the agenda. It is the responsibility of the VCU IRB Leadership and Enhancement Committee Chair to ensure that a fair hearing of the appeal is permitted.
