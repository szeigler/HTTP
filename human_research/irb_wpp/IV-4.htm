Section IV:

IRB Panel Organization, Composition, and Responsibilities

WPP #: IV-4

Title 4:

Responsibilities of  Chairpersons/Vice Chairpersons,  ILEC Chair, and ILEC,

Effective Date:

10-22-13

Revision History:

6-20-00; 11-12-01; 06-07-04; 12-03-04; 04-28-06; 06-21-06; 09-01-09, 10-1-13

Policy Statement

To describe the responsibilities of the ILEC, ILEC Chair,, and Chairpersons/Vice Chairpersons.

Description

The following persons have direct responsibilities on behalf of the VCU IRB, including those described:

  1. Panel Chairpersons/Vice Chairpersons:

    Each Panel Chairperson manages his/her review panel and the matters brought before it according to FDA and DHHS regulations pertaining to the rights and welfare of research subjects. Each Panel Chairperson is responsible for conducting the panel’s meetings in an efficient and orderly fashion with respect given to the opinions of all members. Robert's Rules of Order may be used as a guidebook for conducting the meeting. Each Panel Chairperson serves as primary signatory official for that panel’s IRB correspondence. Should a chairperson not be available to conduct panel business, the vice chairperson serves on his/her behalf. Should neither the chairperson nor the vice chairperson be available, either may designate a senor board member to assume his/her responsibilities during the period of absence.

    Specific responsibilities include:

    Ongoing:

    1. Maintain a thorough understanding of federal regulations pertaining to human subject protections, federal guidance, the VCU IRB Written Policies and Procedures, and other applicable state, and local regulations.
    2. Screen (or defer to the primary reviewer to screen) all on-site SAEs via expedited review to determine which of the following is necessary: (1) take immediate action to address the safety of subjects and/or (2) call an emergency meeting of the Panel or (2) present to the full board at the next scheduled Panel meeting.
    3. 3. Assist with review of SAE/AE and Unexpected problems affecting the safety of subjects as necessary.

    Prior to Each Meeting:

    1. Approve Panel meeting schedule through ORSP.
    2. Coordinate coverage of Vice Chairperson when not able to serve as Chairperson for the meeting (and notify ORSP).
    3. Review items for agenda (Exempt studies, Expedited studies, Full studies, etc.)
    4. Assign Primary and Secondary Reviewers (with appropriate scientific expertise) to studies requiring full Panel review and/or oversee assignments made by panel coordinator.
    5. Assign Reviewer to studies with appropriate scientific expertise requiring Exempt review and or oversee assignments made by panel coordinator.
    6. Assign Reviewer to studies with appropriate scientific expertise requiring Expedited review and/or oversee assignments made by panel coordinator.
    7. Assist the Primary and Secondary Reviewers with any concerns in preparing for the meeting, as necessary.
    8. Assist with the selection of a consultant, as necessary.

    During the IRB Meeting:

    1. Conduct the Panel meeting in an efficient and orderly fashion with respect to all members of the Panel and in accordance with the VCU IRB Written Policies and Procedures (using the support of the ORSP and the OECO Director).
    2. Lead the Primary Reviewer to present a clear and concise overview of the research and discussion of relevant changes and/or questions.
    3. Lead the Secondary Reviewer to present a clear and concise review of any consent documents and subject recruitment materials. Encourage the Secondary Reviewer to record any additional changes required by the Panel directly on his/her copy of the documents where scripted. Non-scripted changes and requested clarification should be noted on the document (where practicable).
    4. Lead the Panel to discuss specific findings, as required by regulation where vulnerable populations are involved, e.g. children, prisoners, pregnant women and fetuses.
    5. Call for a motion for Panel action.
    6. At time of the motion, request that the specific elements pertaining to the motion be clearly repeated for the record.
    7. Ensure that the IRB Coordinator has understood and documented the basis of any motion and vote of the Panel.

    Overall (Leadership Attributes/Expectations):

    1. The ability to conduct meetings of the IRB in an efficient, expeditious and fair manner.
    2. Attentiveness to the details and requirements of the Federal regulations and VCU IRB policies.
    3. Application of the requirements to foster ethically and scientifically sound research.
    4. The ability to set a tone of openness that encourages dialogue in IRB meetings.
    5. Respect for the diverse backgrounds, perspectives and sources of expertise of all IRB members, especially for the contributions of the non-scientists, and the ability to foster such respect among the IRB members.
    6. The confidence and courage to uphold IRB judgments that may not always be popular with principal investigators.
    7. Investment of adequate time, interest and commitment to provide guidance and expertise to IRB members, scientists and others in her/his IC.
  2. IRB Leadership and Enhancement Committee (ILEC):

    The ILEC provides leadership for the VCU IRB system by working to enhance the IRB policies and processes and promoting consistency among IRB Panels. While each IRB panel acts independently for reviews, they are each governed by the same VCU policies and procedures, in addition to state and Federal regulations. This Committee will meet regularly to discuss issues related to the function of the VCU IRBs and their relationship with the research enterprise. It will serve as the central contact point for individual researchers, programs, institutes and the community who inquire about the VCU IRB policies and procedures. The ILEC will strive for consensus among the Chairs and Vice-Chairs in an effort to arrive at consistency, as possible, across Panels. The ILEC will have final approval over adoption of new or substantive revisions of IRB processes, WPPs, submission forms (as they pertain to PI and/or IRB member responsibilities) via the ILEC Chair.

    1. Membership of the ILEC is comprised of the ILEC Chair, IRB Panel Chairs and Vice Chairs, the Directors of the ORSP and ORIE, a representative of the CCTR, and a non-affiliate member of an IRB panel, if possible. In addition, 3 faculty members and one clinical research coordinator with experience conducting human subjects research are appointed to serve on the ILEC. In an effort to provide university-wide representation, these appointments are made by recommendation of the Vice President for Research, Vice President for Health Sciences, and the Vice President for Academic Affairs and Provost.

    2. Responsibilities of the ILEC include:
      1. Maintain and/or enhance the VCU IRB policies and procedures, including WPP’s, forms, documents, and guidelines.
      2. Act as consultants to a panel when specific questions or issues arise within a panel or protocol regarding the WPP’s or regulations.
      3. Quality Assurance and Improvement activities to include, but not limited to:
        1. Assessment of time for review, barriers and delays of review of new, continuing review, and amendments.
        2. Support and review, as needed, post-approval monitoring and findings by the ORIE or other VCU research entities (eg. CCTR, MCC) of human subjects research projects
        3. Initiation of new policies and procedures to enhance IRB processes.
        4. Improvement of information and instruction provided by the IRB on the website.
      4. Appeals by faculty members regarding IRB panel decisions. (See WPP VII-5 Appeal of IRB Decisions).
      5. Assist in identifying informational/knowledge lapses among IRB members, investigators, research staff, students and VCU/VCUHS officials. Support educational efforts in addressing deficits.
  3. The IRB Leadership and Enhancement Committee (ILEC) Chair: The ILEC Chair (formerly, the Senior Chairperson) coordinates chairperson practices, interactions between the IRB Panels and the IRB office and fulfills other responsibilities as the ILEC Chairperson, including:
    1. Serving as a resource for IRB Chairs, IRB Panels, and Researchers
    2. Working closely with the Directors of the ORSP and ORIE, whereby he/she is apprised of developments and needed modifications to the HRPP and advocates for such improvements among the Chairs/Vice-Chairs.
    3. Initiating changes to the HRPP in consultation with the IRB Leadership and Enhancement Committee and the Directors of the ORSP and ORIE.
    4. Serving as ombudsman for concerns, complaints or suggestions that are made to the VCU IRB, and forwarding such issues as needed to the relevant IRB Panel Chair, the IRB Leadership and Enhancement Committee or the Directors of the ORSP and ORIE.
    5. Advising the institutional signatory official of Board activities.
    6. Ensures adequate resources and support to the IRB Boards for research review and identifies areas of need and/or concern.
    7. Arranges for regular review of the IRB policies and practices together with an annual evaluation of the HRPP by the signatory official.
  4. Institutional Support: The ILEC Chair, Panel Chairpersons and Vice Chairpersons and panel members are charged to review protocols and make determinations regarding human subject protections in good faith, according to VCU IRB WPPs and applicable state and federal regulation and institutional policy. In so doing, they shall have the full support of the signatory official and the institution.

Responsibility

It is the responsibility of the Senior Chairperson and Chairpersons/Vice Chairpersons to ensure that the above obligations are met and to report to the ORSP and/or the signatory official of the institution where the need for additional resources arises.
