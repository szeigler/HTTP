WIRB Initial Submissions

What Needs to be Submitted to WIRB?

All industry sponsored research involving human subjects should be submitted to WIRB for review, except projects that meet one or more of the following criteria:

  • involve human subject activities conducted by Navy and Marine Corps personnel;
  • involve naval military personnel and Department of Navy (DoN) employees as research subjects;
  • are supported by naval activities through any agreement (e.g., contract, grant cooperative agreement, development agreement [CRADSs], or other arrangement), regardless of the source of funding, funding appropriation, nature of support, performance site, or security classification; or
  • are using DoN property, facilities or assets.

How do I Submit a New Study to WIRB?

Step 1: Access the appropriate WIRB submission forms. Be sure to complete:

  • All applicable WIRB submission forms,
  • the VCU Cover Memo, and
  • the VCU-WIRB Personnel Roster

Step 2: Submit all materials electronically via email to the Clinical Trials Office (CTO) at CTOadmin@vcu.edu. If you need assistance scanning paper documents to PDF files, scanning capabilities are available for your use at the CTO (Richmond Academy of Medicine Building, 1st Floor, 1200 E. Clay Street). Direct questions to the CTO at 804-628-2942. CTO will review the study budget and consent form. When the submission is complete and ready to move forward, CTO will forward submission materials to the Office of Research Subjects Protection (ORSP).

Step 3: Once the submission arrives at ORSP, Conflict of Interest and training requirements will be checked. As the PI, be sure to respond to any requests for additional information from ORSP.

Step 4: When all submission requirements are met, ORSP will upload all electronic submission documents to WIRB and notify the PI that the submission has been done.

Step 5: Respond to any requests for additional information from WIRB to secure approval.

Central IRB Initial Submissions

What studies may be submitted to the NCI Central IRB?

Cooperative Cancer Group sponsored studies that are already approved by the NCI Central IRB (CIRB) are eligible for a facilitated local review process by the VCU IRB. The Principal Investigator or regulatory coordinator is responsible for determining that a study is eligible.

How do I submit an Initial Review for Facilitated Review?

  1. Visit the Central IRB Website: http://www.ncicirb.org/
  2. Click on Participant’s Area and log in using the user name and password provided by CIRB. If you do not have a user name and password, contact Michelle Stickler, Director ORSP at mcstickler@vcu.edu.
  3. Use the search function to find the study you wish to submit for review
  4. Download the following documents for the study:
    1. Initial approval letter
    2. Cooperative Group summary of progress, if applicable
    3. Current Continuing Review approval letter
    4. Currently Approved Protocol and approval letter for this version
    5. Notification letters, if applicable
    6. DSMB Reports, if available
    7. Investigator Drug Brochure
    8. Primary reviews for initial review and most recent continuing review
    A listing of all study documents to select for download may be accessed by clicking on the box that says, “To download the Local IRB Facilitated Review Packet click here”
  5. Complete the VCU Application for Facilitated Review
  6. Generate a local consent document that addresses:
    1. Local PI contact information
    2. VCU injury clause language
    3. Any local study procedures differing from the standard protocol
  7. Generate local recruitment materials following VCU IRB guidelines
  8. Prepare complete submission package including:
    1. VCU Application for Facilitated Review
    2. CIRB supporting documents
    3. PRMS approval
    4. Personnel roster and information sheets
    5. Local consent form
    6. Local recruitment materials
  9. Submit two hard copies and one electronic copy of all documents to the Office of Research Subjects Protection. Email PDF documents to IRBIntake@vcu.edu and IRBPanelA@vcu.edu with Central IRB Facilitated Review Submission clearly indicated in the subject line.
    1. If more than a single email is required to send all attachments, the subject line of the second email should include: PI last name, Cooperative Group #, and Part # (e.g., part 1 or part 2)
    2. Email attachments should be named with a title that makes the nature of the document evident.
