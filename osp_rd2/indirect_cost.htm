Costs that are incurred for common or joint objectives and, therefore, cannot be identified readily and specifically with a particular sponsored project, an instructional activity, or any other institutional activity. The term Facilities & Administration ("F&A") Costs is synonymous with Indirect Costs ("IDC") and are sometimes called "overhead".

Examples of indirect costs include: library services; payroll, purchasing, human resources and other centrally-provided services; administrative and clerical costs; building use and maintenance, to name a few. F&A costs are calculated as a percentage of direct costs. These F&A rates are set through negotiation between the university and its cognizant agency in the federal government. VCU's cognizant agency is the Department of Health and Human Services (DHHS).

Determine from your sponsor's guidelines whether F&A costs should be calculated based on "Total Direct Costs" (TDC) or "Modified Total Direct Costs" (MTDC). TDC includes all direct costs. MDTC excludes some direct costs from the calculation base. Our negotiated rate agreement defines which direct cost items should be excluded on an MTDC budget. MTDC shall exclude: equipment; subcontracts in excess of $25,000, tuition, patient care costs, rental of off-site facilities, capital expenditures, fellowships and scholarships. If not specifically excluded, all direct costs including the first $25,000 of each subcontract are subject to F&A.

Indirect costs apply to all types of sponsored projects (grants, contracts, cooperative agreements) funded by federal, state or private sponsors. F&A cost rates will vary according to whether the project is research, instruction or other and whether 50% or more of the project is performed on or off campus. Consult the DHHS rate agreement for current rates, and contact OSP for updates and questions. Please note that the industry-sponsored clinical trial rate is a fixed percentage of TDC.
