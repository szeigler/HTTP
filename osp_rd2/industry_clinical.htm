Industry-funded Clinical Trial Budget

Most industry-funded clinical trials have "two" budgets, the external sponsor budget based on fixed unit costs and the internal budget based on estimated itemized expenses. The total costs for the two budgets should be equal and include both direct and indirect costs. Industry sponsors will not generally include personnel costs as a line item however these expenses must be accounted for in the internal budget. An industry-funded clinical trial budget has an F&A rate of 30% of Total Direct Costs (TDC).

There is a single period of performance for most industry-funded clinical trials, i.e. a start date and a projected end date but not annualized start and end dates. The projected end date can be foreshortened or extended based on the actual full duration of the trial.

Industry-funded clinical trial budgets are comprised of fixed unit costs that are paid on a per patient basis once certain deliverables and/or milestones have been reached. A clinical trial budget is always a rough estimate because, at the project's outset, we can only make a best guess about how many study subjects will actually be enrolled in the trial, and/or will complete the agreed upon milestones. For this reason, G&C tracks industry-funded clinical trials on a cash accrual basis which means that dollars are loaded to your index based on revenue received.

An Industry-funded clinical trial internal budget can include the same direct cost categories as any other sponsored project budget, i.e. personnel salaries and fringe, supplies, travel, etc. The industry-funded fixed unit cost budget includes fixed unit per patient costs as well as reimbursable "invoiceable" fees such as start up, IRB review, storage, adverse event reporting, screen failures, and ancillary services as described below:

  • Start up: VCU needs to capture the costs of the start-up efforts by our faculty and staff. This is a nonrefundable fee that should be paid whether we enroll any study subjects or not.
  • IRB review: VCU uses Western Institutional Review Board (WIRB) as the central IRB for all industry-funded projects involving human subjects. Ideally, WIRB will bill the Sponsor directly. If the Sponsor cannot agree to pay WIRB directly, F&A rates will accrue on all WIRB invoices processed by VCU. Be sure to account for initial review, annual review, and protocol amendments if WIRB is billing VCU.
  • Adverse event reporting: Be sure to include Sponsor reimbursement of fees associated with reporting of adverse events to the Sponsor and to the IRB.
  • Storage: Include payment for long-term storage of study documents.
  • Screen failures: Sponsors should pay for a certain number of "screen failures", that is potential study subjects who do not meet the inclusion criteria, because VCU personnel effort is expended in the screening process.
  • Ancillary services: Clinical trials routinely use the Pharmacy, Pathology, Radiology, and other "ancillary services." The budget should reflect the cost of these services based on the ancillary service agreement.
