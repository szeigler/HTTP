Transfer a Grant to VCU (from another institution)

Through the process of proposal submission and award issuance, an Institution and sponsor make a binding legal commitment to one another. When a PI moves from one institution to another there is a formal process to amend the commitment to reflect the new recipient instiution. Transfer of a sponsored project from one institution to another typically involves two steps, i.e. (1) formal "relinquishment" or "giving up" by the institution that received the grant, contract or coorperative agreement, and (2) a "transfer application" submitted by the new institution and approved in writing by the sponsor.

When a PI on a grant is leaving an institution, that entity may either nominate a replacement who is equally or better qualified OR relinquish the grant. If the grant is transferring to VCU, OSP must receive evidence that the PI's former institution is willing to release the sponsored project, typically in the form of a signed letter or copy of the relinquishment notice. Change of institution may involve the transfer of equipment purchased with grant funds.

Many sponsors provide detailed instructions on the steps needed to transfer a grant. It is the PI's responsibility to work closely with OSP and other units to complete all required steps. Incoming transferred projects are "new" to VCU and should be prepared as would any incoming proposal with IAF, AIRS reporting, and all of the forms required by the sponsor.  Additionally, OSP requires a completed "Grant Transfer Between Institutions" form. Other units that might be involved include the Office of Subjects Protection (human and/or animal subjects), Innovation Gateway (technology transfer) and the Office of Environmental Safety (recombinant DNA, hazardous agents, radioactive materials.) Documentation must also be provided for any subrecipients proposed to be involved in the project.

The sponsor will evaluate the transfer application to (1) determine if there is a continued need for the grant-supported project; (2) evaluate the impact of relocation on approved scope of work; (3) confirm that facilities and resources at new institution will allow for successful performance of the project. Requests to change grantee organization should be made before the anticipated start date at the new organization and preferably several months in advance. Failure to provide timely notification may result in disapproval of the request or a delay in processing.
