"Streaming Potential Device" VCU #13-055

Applications

  • Measurement of streaming potentials for polymer coatings and film
  • New field for surface characterization of adsorption phenomena
  • Can be applied to both chemical sensors and biosensors

Advantages

  • Improves accuracy when measuring streaming potential of surfaces
  • No need for calibration or reference electrode maintenance
  • Eliminates voltage drift

Market Need

The measurement of zeta potentials is common for colloids. However, reliable determinations of zeta potentials for thin films and coatings are problematic. Current devices that utilize zeta potential measurements lack the ability to accurately and precisely analyze a substrate or a solution since the solution is measured separately from the control. Since there is variability when measuring the solution and control sequentially, there exists a need to allow parallel measurements.

Technology Summary

This technology is a novel device that allows parallel measurements, thereby improving both accuracy and precision significantly. This device utilizes a dual detection system allowing two streams to be measured at once. With this dual channel system, there is a uniform clamping force throughout both channels allowing both improvement in the deviceâ€™s operation and accuracy in the measurement. Another improvement is that this device allows for real-time capture of zeta potential measurements which is beneficial during chemical analysis, surface charge determination and/or patient monitoring. This technology uses non-reference electrodes in order to reduce the need for calibration and maintenance, as well as utilizing pulsed flow to generate streaming potentials with minimal drift that could occur from using non-reference electrodes. Ultimately, this new method permits measurement of zeta potentials on coatings and thin films in parallel with that of a control; therefore, allowing real-time measurements to be taken that are capable of monitoring adsorption of biological components within physiological fluids and assessing quality of polymer coatings and films.

Technology Status

This technology has been prototyped.

Patent Pending: U.S. and Foreign rights available.

This technology is available for licensing to industry for further development and commercialization.
