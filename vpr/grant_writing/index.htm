New Investigators Grant Writing Institutes: 2015-2016 CAREER Academy

The Virginia Commonwealth University New Investigators Grant Writing Institutes (GWI) provide year-long, intensive proposal development training for VCU faculty from across the university who will apply for extramural funding within 12 months. Funded by the VCU Office of the Vice President for Research and Innovation, the GWI is run from Fall to early Summer and changes focus each year.

The 2015-2016 Grant Writing Institute will be a CAREER Academy, targeting faculty who will be applying to the National Science Foundation Faculty Early Career Development Program (CAREER) in 2016 and 2017.

The CAREER Academy will bring together an interdisciplinary cohort of up to 10 faculty to: build skills in competitive proposal development, learn and practice effective communication to diverse audiences, and participate in on-going critical review.

On-line applications to participate in the 2015-2016 CAREER Academy are due by 5:00pm on June 15, 2015.

Details [+][-]

The CAREER Academy offers several training modules that, when combined over a period of time, aim to steadily build understanding and skills in competitive grantsmanship.

  • Intensive Proposal Development Workshops: The cohort participates in monthly group meetings that provide step-by-step guidance developing competitive CAREER proposals; research and project design; persuasive communication and writing; grant writing; grant submission; and building and maintaining relationships with the funder.
  • Peer review: Each CAREER Academy participant is required to serve as a Primary and Secondary reviewer to other cohort members, and give meaningful feedback about their peers’ proposal drafts at several stages of development throughout the year. This process trains investigators in how to give and receive critique, and in how to better understand the psychology of the grant reviewer.
  • Mock review: Going beyond the peer review, the Mock Review allows participants to receive critical feedback on their proposals from both internal and external content matter experts. Further, this is a dynamic learning experience. Unlike a true review panel meeting, the investigator has the opportunity to respond to reviewers’ comments, ask questions, and make edits before the upcoming deadline as appropriate.
  • Scientific Mentoring: Each CAREER Academy cohort member is matched with a scientific mentor, who will provide ongoing guidance and review of the research plan’s intellectual merit. Mentors receive a modest stipend.
  • Writing Labs: As a continuation of one of the 2014-2015 cohort's own initiatives, Writing Labs offer CAREER Academy faculty scheduled time, dedicated quiet space, mutual support, and facilitation as needed to focus on writing projects -- including publications and other grants.
  • Targeted Resources: An extensive collection of resources is available to cohort members through a limited-access Google site and shared folders to facilitate collaboration between cohort members. Prior CAREER Academy participants and awardees have generously added their proposals and reviewer comments to the collection. The site also includes unfunded proposals, reflections from CAREER applicants, advice and presentations from program officers, articles, webinars, and much more.
  • Individualized Support & Technical Assistance: Each cohort member receives one-on-one attention and assistance. As their research and proposals develop, take new paths, and accelerate at different rates, facilitators to provide guidance, support and encouragement.

Schedule [+][-]

The 2015-2016 CAREER Academy will take place from August 2015 (orientation meeting) through July 2016. Cohort meetings will take place once a month, 1-4pm, for intensive proposal development training. Participants are expected to attend all scheduled cohort meetings throughout the year. Cohort members should adopt a regular meeting schedule with their mentors.

Participants may also elect to meet with each other, instructors and facilitators, or attend other related workshops at other times to supplement their training.

Selection & Expectations [+][-]

In order to participate in the 2015-2016 CAREER Academy, faculty must meet the eligibility criteria to serve as Principal Investigators for the 2016 NSF CAREER award. Please see the grant eligibility guidelines and Frequently Asked Questions for further details.

Priority will be given to faculty who:

  • Are tenure-track, Assistant Professors.
  • Plan to apply for the NSF CAREER award in July 2016.
  • Have not previously participated in the VCU Grant Writing Institute or CAREER Academy.
  • Demonstrate some record of scholarly publication, presentation, or creative expression.
  • Demonstrate some record of grant writing experience (funded or unfunded).

Specific attention will be placed bringing together individuals from diverse disciplines to build a cohort that is dynamic, creative, good-humored, and mutually supportive and accountable. As such, all on-line applications will be followed up with brief (15-20 minute) telephone calls with interested faculty before final selections are made.

The CAREER Academy is limited to 10 participants in the 2015-2016 cohort.

Expectations of CAREER Academy cohort members:

In order to participate in the CAREER Academy, faculty must commit to:

  • Participate in all working sessions.
  • Actively engage with cohort peers.
  • Participate fully in all peer review activities. Submit proposal drafts several days prior to each monthly working sessions. As Primary or Secondary reviewers, provide peers with thorough and critical oral and written feedback on proposal drafts throughout the year.
  • Participate fully in all mock review activities.
  • Complete all assignments and meet milestones.
  • Actively engage with mentor.
  • Update CAREER Academy facilitators regularly on progress, and provide ongoing feedback on program usefulness.
  • Provide feedback for the program evaluation.

Participation in planned CAREER Academy activities, as well as out-of-class proposal preparation, is estimated at 10 hours per month. Cohort members who miss more than one scheduled session will be required to meet with facilitators to re-assess their participation.

Expectations of CAREER Academy mentors:

Upon being accepted into the CAREER Academy, mentors will be expected to:

  • Meet regularly with the mentee to review and refine scientific plan.
  • Review the mentee’s proposal drafts. Provide oral and written feedback.
  • Help identify and secure necessary resources to improve the competitiveness of the science.
  • Consult with CAREER Academy in organizing Mock Review panels.
  • Provide feedback for the program evaluation.

Mentor participation is estimated at 5 hours per month. Each mentor will receive $300.00 in a departmental account for their active participation.

Application [+][-]

Click here to apply for the 2015-2016 CAREER Academy.

The application consists of the following 4 brief parts:

  1. Applicant Background Information: Provide your contact information; past experience with the NSF CAREER Program; and a brief description of your past grantwriting experience.
  2. Plans for NSF CAREER Proposal Development: Provide a short list of keywords; a brief abstract of your planned NSF CAREER proposal; a short personal statement describing why you would like to participate in the VCU CAREER Academy.
  3. Mentor Information: Provide contact information for your scientific mentor.
  4. Support Documents: Upload the following:
    • NSF-style biosketch, 2 pages maximum and saved as a PDF. Instructions for preparing the biographical sketch are outlined in the Grant Proposal Guide. Files should be named: lastname_careerbiosketch2015.pdf.
    • The signed Attestations and Endorsements Form, saved as a PDF. Files should be named: lastname_careerattest2015.pdf.

Application Deadline

CAREER Academy applications are due by 5:00pm on June 15, 2015. All applications must be submitted online with the biographical sketch and signed Attestations and Endorsements Form attached.
