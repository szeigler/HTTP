The New Investigators’ Grant Writing Institute

Please direct all questions about the New Investigators’ Grant Writing Institute:

  • Ann Nichols-Casebolt, PhD
    Senior Associate Vice President for Research Development, Office of Research and Innovation
    acasebol@vcu.edu
  • Jessica Venable
    Coordinator of Research Development Services, Office of Research and Innovation
    jcvenable@vcu.edu

The New Investigators’ Grant Writing Institute (the Institute) provides year-long, intensive proposal development training for VCU faculty from across the university who will apply for extramural funding within 12 months.

Funded by the VCU Office of the Vice President for Research and Innovation, the Institute is run annually and changes focus each year.

The 2014-2015 Institute will focus on building skills in proposal development and interdisciplinary communication – both of which are critical to competitive grantsmanship. It will convene an interdisciplinary cohort of new investigators committed to building their grant writing skills, communicating to diverse audiences, and participating in on-going peer review.

Applications to participate in the 2014-2015 New Investigators’ Grant Writing Institute are due by 5:00pm on August 4, 2014.

Details [+][-]

By participating in this intensive group training, it is expected that researchers will also learn about science across the spectrum of disciplines, and come to a better understanding of how to work collaboratively. The As such, it will offer a combination of modules:

  • Intensive grant writing workshops: These sessions will focus on providing step-by-step guidance on grant writing, including Finding the Right Funder; Constructing Specific Aims/Goals & Objectives; Crafting Page 1; Communicating with Program Officers; Conceptualizing the Budget; Identifying VCU Resources; and others. These workshops will begin with a two-day event with guest presenter Dr. Robert Porter, national expert on competitive proposal development.
  • Peer review: In order to encourage continual writing and growth in your communication style, peer review is a critical component to the Grant Writing Institute. Each participant will be responsible for providing meaningful critiques of the other cohort members’ writing and presentations at a number of stages of development throughout the year.
  • Mock reviews: Going beyond the peer review, the Mock Review allows participants to receive critical feedback on their proposals from both internal and external content matter experts. Further, this is a dynamic learning experience. Unlike a true review panel meeting, the investigator (and others in the cohort) will be present at the mock review session. This format gives investigators the opportunity to respond to reviewers’ comments, ask questions, and make critical edits before the upcoming deadline as appropriate. Mock reviews will take place multiple times.
  • Agency visits: Participants will arrange meetings with their cognizant Program Officers in Washington, DC, funded by the program.
  • Mentoring: Participants will be matched with a scientific mentor, who will provide additional guidance and review. Mentors will receive a modest stipend.

Schedule [+][-]

The 2014-2015 New Investigators’ Grant Writing Institute will take place from September 2014 (kick off meeting) through July 2015. Planned sessions will take place once a month, although participants may elect to meet with each other, meet with Institute instructors and facilitators, or attend other related workshops at other times to supplement their learning.

Participants are expected to attend all sessions throughout the year, even if their proposals are due before July 2015.

Selection & Expectations [+][-]

Selection of participants

Priority will be given to faculty who:

  1. Are tenure-track, Assistant Professors.
  2. Demonstrate some record of scholarly publication, presentation, or creative expression.
  3. Demonstrate some record of grant writing experience (funded or unfunded).
  4. Plan to submit a proposal for extramural funding no later than Summer 2015.

Selection will be made with attention to create a cohort of faculty from diverse disciplines.

The Institute is limited to 10 participants in the 2014-2015 cohort.

Expectations of participants

In order to participate in the Institute, faculty must commit to:

  • Participate in all working sessions.
  • Actively engage with cohort peers. Participate fully all in peer and mock review activities.
  • Complete all assignments and meet milestones.
  • Actively engage with mentor.
  • Provide feedback for the program evaluation.

As participation in planned Institute activities as well as out-of-class proposal preparation, estimated at 10 hours per month, all participations will have to secure a departmental endorsement. This endorsement assures that the investigator will receive necessary release time to participate.

Selection of mentors

In order to participate in the Institute, mentors must:

  1. Have scientific/content matter expertise in the mentee’s research area being proposed.
  2. Be familiar with interests, funding priorities, and review criteria of the funding agency the mentee is targeting. Mentors will, preferably, have a history of funding and/or served as reviewers for that agency.
  3. Agree to provide one-on-one guidance to the mentee during the 2014-2015 academic year in the development of a proposal to submit in Summer 2015.
  4. Complete and sign the “Attestations and Endorsements” form of the mentee’s New Investigators’ Grant Writing Institute application form. Both the applicant and the mentor must be accepted to the 2014-2015 cohort.

Expectations of mentors

Upon being accepted into the Grant Writing Institute, mentors will also be expected to:

  • Attend the Kick-off reception.
  • Communicate regularly with the mentee.
  • Review the mentee’s proposal drafts. Provide oral and written feedback.
  • Help the mentee identify collaborators, resources, and skills that the mentee needs to be competitive.
  • Consult with Institute facilitators in organizing Mock Review panels.
  • Provide feedback for the program evaluation.

Mentor participation is estimated at 5 hours per month. Therefore, all mentors will have to secure a departmental endorsement on the application. This endorsement assures that the investigator will receive necessary release time to participate. Mentors will also receive $300.00 in a departmental account for their participation.

Application [+][-]

Apply for the 2014-2015 New Investigators’ Grant Writing Institute here.

On-line Applications to the 2014-2015 New Investigators’ Grant Writing Institute consist of four (4) brief parts.

  1. Participant Information. Include your contact information and past grant writing experience.
  2. Proposed Plan for Extramural Funding (keywords and short personal statement). You may want to compose your personal statement in a word processing application, and cut and paste the narrative into the space provided.
  3. Mentor Information.
  4. Support Documents. Upload the following on page 4 of the on-line application.
    • Biographical sketch. For guidance on the biographical sketch, you should follow the NIH, NSF, or NEH Fellowships format. The biographical sketch should be saved as a PDF and named “lastname_gwinstbiosketch2014.pdf”.
    • Attestations and Endorsements Form. A form outlining the terms and conditions of participation in the Institute must be signed by both the proposed faculty participant and the mentor. It must also be endorsed by the department, by way of the department chair/designee’s signature. Once all signatures are secured, the document must be saved as a PDF and named “lastname_gwinstattest2014.pdf”.

Application Deadlines

Applications are due by 5:00pm on August 4, 2014. All applications must be submitted online with the biographical sketch and signed Attestations and Endorsements Form attached.

Applicants and mentors will be notified of selection decisions via email by August 15, 2014.

2013-2014 CAREER Academy [+][-]

The Office of Research and Innovation launched its Grant Writing Institutes effort in 2013-2014 with the CAREER Academy. Its purpose was to provide proposal development support to VCU faculty from across the university who were interested applying to the NSF CAREER Program in 2014 or 2015.

The National Science Foundation’s (NSF) Faculty Early Career Development (CAREER) Program awards are among the most prestigious and competitive NSF awards, which typically support a five-year, $400,000 research program. The CAREER award is intended for faculty members who are at or near the beginning of their careers.

The CAREER Academy provided a portfolio of support and resources to the faculty participants that include step-by-step guidance on developing competitive CAREER proposals, oral and written feedback on proposal drafts, one-on-one mentoring with senior faculty and a mock review panel.

A group of 11 VCU faculty members were selected to participate in the 2013-2014 CAREER Academy.

The CAREER Academy was funded by the VCU Office of the Vice President for Research and Innovation, and supported by the College for Humanities and Sciences and School of Education.
