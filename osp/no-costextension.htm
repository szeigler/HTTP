No-cost Extension Requests

In an effort to provide a simpler way of handling no-cost extension requests, OSPA is implementing the following process for no-cost extension requests:

How to Obtain a No-Cost Extension

Principal Investigators often need more time than originally anticipated to complete work on a grant. Requesting a no-fund (no additional funds), time-only extension (commonly referred to as a no-cost extension or NCE) is the appropriate mechanism for achieving this.

In order to comply with federal regulations regarding requests for extensions, no-cost extension requests must be received by the Office of Sponsored Programs at least 15 days prior to the currently approved end date.

For an extension to a National Science Foundation grant, go to NSF FastLane

Log in and follow the instructions for no-cost extensions under Research Administration – Notifications & Requests.

For all other sponsors:

The Principal Investigator should prepare an e-mail addressed to:
dirospa@vcu.edu. In order to comply with current federal regulations, the e-mail message must originate from the PI. Proxy submissions will not be accepted. A copy should be sent to the PI's fiscal administrator.

The e-mail should include the following information:

  • Sponsor Name
  • Sponsor Award Number (and SPA Number, if available)
  • Current End Date
  • New Requested End Date

Justification for Extension - Provide information explaining why an extension is needed. Please note: An extension cannot be exercised merely for the purpose of using unobligated balances or to bridge potential funding gaps due to competing cycles.

Submit the e-mail to the above address. The principal investigator and fiscal administrator will receive notification of either: 1) approval of the extension (certain federal agencies permit OSPA electronic notification and immediate approval) or 2) formal submission of a request to the Sponsor.

Updates of pending requests will be provided as received from the Sponsor.

This process should suffice for nearly every request; however, as we encounter problems and gain experience, we may need to revise or amend it. Please let us know if you have any problems, questions, or concerns. It is our hope to have this posted to our website in the near future.
